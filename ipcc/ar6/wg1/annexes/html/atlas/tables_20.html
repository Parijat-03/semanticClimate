3 The main objective of the CORDEX-CORE initiative is to provide a world-wide homogeneous foundation of
4 high-resolution regional climate model projections to improve the understanding of local phenomena and to
5 allow impact and adaptation research world-wide (Gutowski et al., 2016). The experimental framework was
6 designed to produce homogeneous regional projections for most of the land regions and, therefore, it is
7 requested that the contributing RCMs run projections over most CORDEX domains at 0.22° resolution
8 driven by the same core set of GCMs for the two scenarios RCP2.6 and RCP8.5 (see Box Atlas.1, Figure 1:).
9 Due to the computational requirements, three GCMs were selected to drive the simulations, covering the
10 spread of high, medium, and low (HADGEM2ES, MPI-ESM, and NorESM, respectively) equilibrium
11 climate sensitivity from the CMIP5 ensemble at a global scale (using MIROC5, EC-Earth, GFDL-ES2M as
12 secondary GCMs). Two RCMs have contributed so far to this initiative (REMO and RegCM4) constituting
13 an initial homogeneous downscaled ensemble to analyze mean climate change signals and hazards (Coppola
14 et al., submittedb; Teichmann et al., submitted; see also Chapter 12), and there are ongoing efforts to extend
15 the CORDEX-CORE ensemble with additional regional simulations (e.g. the COSMO-CLM community) to
16 further increase the ensemble size and the representation of possible future climate change pathways.
