For 3 example, melt rate biases are prevalent throughout most simulations of NA-CORDEX RCMs (HIRHAM5, 4 RCA4, RegCM4, CRCM5, CanRCM4 and WRF) over the Sierra Nevada, and all GCM-driven RCM 5 simulations have a shorter total snow season length (on average, 44 fewer days) when compared with SNSR. 6 7 Rain-on-snow characteristics from the CRCM5 and CanRCM4 models have been assessed by Jeong and 8 Sushama (2018). When driven by ERA-Interim, the two RCM simulations capture reasonably well the 9 spatial distributions of the observed rain-on-snow days; however, they tend to overestimate the observed 10 frequency, particularly over the central parts of North America.
