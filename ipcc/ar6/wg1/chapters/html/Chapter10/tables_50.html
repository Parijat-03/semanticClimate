1 adjustment for downscaling has been shown to artificially modify long-term trends, misrepresent the spatial characteristics of extreme\nevents, and misrepresent local weather phenomena such as temperature inversions (Maraun, 2013a; Gutmann et al., 2014; Maraun\net al., 2017). Crucially, sub-grid influences on the local climate change signal are not represented. For instance, if a mountain chain is\nnot resolved in the driving model, the snow–albedo feedback is not represented by the bias adjustment such that local temperature\ntrends in high altitudes are under-represented (Cross-Chapter Box 10.2, Figure 1; Maraun et al., 2017). It has therefore been suggested\nto account for local random variability by combining bias adjustment with stochastic downscaling (Volosciuk et al., 2017; Lange,\n2019), although this approach still does not account for local modifications of the climate change signal. Two approaches have been\nproposed to represent these local changes: dynamical downscaling with high-resolution RCMs (Maraun et al., 2017) or statistical\nemulators of such (Walton et al., 2015).
