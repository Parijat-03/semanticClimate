Given the dual challenges of rising urban GHG emissions and future projections of more frequent 18 extreme climate events, there is an urgent need to integrate urban mitigation and adaptation 19 strategies for cities to address climate change (very high confidence). Mitigation strategies can 20 enhance resilience against climate change impacts while contributing to social equity, public health, 21 and human well-being. Urban mitigation actions that facilitate economic decoupling can have positive 22 impacts on employment and local economic competitiveness. {8.2, Cross-Working Group Box 2 in 23 Chapter 8, 8.4} 24
Cities can achieve net zero or near net zero GHG emissions only through deep decarbonisation 25 and systemic transformation (very high confidence). Effective emission reductions in cities entail 26 implementing three broad strategies concurrently: (1) reducing urban energy consumption across all 27 sectors, including through compact and efficient urban forms and supporting infrastructure; (2) 28 electrification and switching to low carbon energy sources; and (3) enhancing carbon uptake and stocks 29 (medium evidence, high agreement). Given the regional and global reach of urban supply chains, a city 30 cannot achieve net zero GHG emissions by only focusing on reducing emissions within its 31 administrative boundaries. {8.1.6, 8.3.4, 8.4, 8.6} 32
Packages of mitigation policies that implement multiple urban-scale interventions can have 33 cascading effects across sectors, reduce GHG emissions outside a city’s administrative 34 boundaries, and reduce emissions more than the net sum of individual interventions, particularly 35 if multiple scales of governance are included (high confidence). Cities have the ability to implement 36 policy packages across sectors using an urban systems approach, especially those that affect key 37 infrastructure based on spatial planning, electrification of the urban energy system, and urban green and 38 blue infrastructure. The institutional capacity of cities to develop, coordinate, and integrate sectoral 39 mitigation strategies within their jurisdiction varies by context, particularly those related to governance, 40 the regulatory system, and budgetary control. {8.4, 8.5, 8.6} 41
