Recently, warm events in the West Antarctica associated with drizzle were observed (Nicolas et al., 26 2017; Wille et al., 2019). In the current climate, most rain falling on the Antarctic continent refreezes in the 27 snowpack due to the low temperature and thus also contributes to ice-sheet mass accumulation (Agosta et al., 28 2019; Lenaerts et al., 2019). 29 30 Direct observations of snowfall using traditional gauges are highly uncertain due to strong winds and 31 blowing snow. Only a few stations have started to be equipped with precipitation radars (Gorodetskaya et al., 32 2015; Grazioli et al., 2017) with records still not long enough to allow for the assessment of trends. Trends in 33 precipitation cannot be assessed with reanalyses because spurious changes are introduced by the increase in 34 assimilated satellite observations (Nicolas and Bromwich, 2011).
