The feasibility of mitigation scenarios 1
Scenarios provide internally consistent projections of emission reduction drivers and help contextualize 2 the scale of deployment and interactions of mitigation strategies. Recent research has proposed and 3 operationalised frameworks for the feasibility assessment of mitigation scenarios. In this report the 4 feasibility assessment of scenarios uses an approach that involves developing a set of multi-dimensional 5 metrics capturing the timing, disruptiveness and the scale of the transformative change within five 6 dimensions: geophysical, technological, economic, socio-cultural and institutional, as illustrated in 7 Box TS.15 Figure 1. 8
More than 20 indicators were chosen to represent feasibility dimensions that could be related to scenario 9 metrics. Thresholds of feasibility risks of different intensity were obtained through empirical analysis 10 of historical data and assessed literature. Details of indicators, thresholds, and how they were applied is 11 reported in Annex II Part IV Section 11. {3.8} 12

18 A wide range of factors have been found to enable sustainability transitions, ranging from 19 technological innovations to shifts in markets, and from policies and governance arrangements 20 to shifts in belief systems and market forces (high confidence). Many of these factors have come 21 together in a co-evolutionary process that has unfolded globally, internationally and locally over several 22 decades (low evidence, high agreement). Those same conditions that may serve to impede the transition 23 (i.e., organisational structure, behaviour, technological lock-in) can also ‘flip’ to enable both the 24 transition and the framing of sustainable development policies to create a stronger basis for policy 25 support (high confidence). It is important to note that strong shocks to these systems, including 26 accelerating climate change impacts, economic crises and political changes, may provide crucial 27 openings for accelerated transitions to sustainable systems. For example, re-building more sustainably 28 after an extreme event, or renewed public debate about the drivers of social and economic vulnerability 29 to multiple stressors (medium confidence). {17.4} 30
