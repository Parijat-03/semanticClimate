4

5

6
1.5°C scenario, or two thirds of the carbon budget in the 2°C scenario (Tong et al. 2019). Kefford et al.
7

8

9

10

11

12
(Ansari and Holz 2020) with an impact on workers, local communities, companies and governments
13

14

15

Box 17.1 Case study: coal transitions
34
35
The coal transition will pose challenges not only to the power sector, but even more importantly to coal-
36
mining. A less diversified local economy, low labour mobility and heavy dependence on coal revenues
37
will make closing down coal production particularly challenging from a political economy perspective.
38
Policy is needed to support and invest in impacted areas to smooth the transition, absorb the impact and
39
incentivize new opportunities. A supportive policy for the transition could include both short-term
40
support and long-term investment. Short-term compensation could be helpful for local workers,
41
communities, companies and governments to manage the consequences of coal closures. Earlier
42
involvement with local stakeholders using a structured approach is crucial and will make the transition
43
policy more targeted and better administered. The long-term policy should target support to the local
44
economy and workers to move beyond coal, including a strategic plan to transform the impacted area,
45
investment in local infrastructure and education, and preference policies to incentivize emerging
46
businesses. Most importantly, ex-ante policy implementation is far better than ex-post compensation.
47
Even without the climate imperative, historical evidence shows that coal closures can happen
48
surprisingly fast.
