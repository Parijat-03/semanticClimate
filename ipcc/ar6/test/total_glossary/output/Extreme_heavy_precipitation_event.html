extreme/heavy precipitation event

An extreme/heavy precipitation event is an event that is of very high magnitude with a very rare occurrence at a particular place.

Types of extreme precipitation may vary depending on its duration, hourly, daily or multi-days (e.g., 5 days), though all of them qualitatively represent high magnitude. The intensity of such events may be defined with a block maxima approach such as annual maxima or with peaks over threshold approach, such as rainfall above the 95th or 99th percentile at a particular place.
