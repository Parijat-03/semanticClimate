17.4
Key barriers and enablers of the transition: synthesizing results
35
This section provides a deep and broad synthesis of theory (section 17.2) and evidence (section 17.3)
36
in order to identify the conditions that either enable or inhibit transitions to sustainable low-carbon
37
futures. Following the literature on sustainability transitions (see Cross-chapter Box 12 on Transition
38
Dynamics), the section finds that there is rarely any one single factor promoting or preventing such
39
transitions. Rather, marked departures from business as usual typically involve several factors,
40
including technological innovations, shifts in markets, concerted efforts by scientists and civil-society
41
organizations to raise awareness of the costs of continued emissions, social movements, policies and
42
governance arrangements, and changes in belief systems and values.
43
44
All of this comes together in a co-evolutionary process that has unfolded globally, internationally and
45
locally over several decades (Hansen and Nygaard 2014; Rogge et al. 2017; Sorman et al. 2020), and
46
that may be guided or facilitated by interventions that target leverage points in the underlying
47
development path (Burch and Di Bella 2021; Leventon et al. 2021). While transitions necessarily follow
48
context-specific trajectories, more general lessons can be drawn by comparing the empirical details with
49
both system-level and narrower explanations of change.
