0 Vulnerable Communities
1 • The Department will commission community resilience assessments in vulnerable areas to understand the needs\nof residents.\n• The Department will work closely with ACs and ward-level climate action officers to develop community\nresilience action plans at the local area level, address infrastructure and service delivery gaps and downscale DRR\nprotocol to best fit community needs.\n• The Department will work closely with SRA, MHADA and other planning agencies influencing housing design and\nallocation to ensure resilient infrastructure is built.\n• The Department will work closely with the informal or formal labor groups employed in high-risk or vulnerable\nworkforces (such as auto/taxi drivers exposed to heat risk and construction labor) and unhoused populations to\naddress their adaptation needs.

0 Innovations & Green Finance
1 • The Department will maintain a repository of solutions, technologies and tools to integrate the right solutions\nwith the right projects or plans within the cell (similar to the GoI’s AGNI mission that brings local solutions to meet\nlocal needs).\n• The Department will work with other departments within the CAC and across BMC to identify needs and\nchallenges and connect the right solutions.\n• The Department will work towards leveraging green finance to increase the overall finance pool, attract\ncutting-edge innovations and help channelize CSR/philanthropic funding and public private partnerships.\n• The Department will also host a sustainability tech-fest to promote solutions and technologies to meet Mumbai’s\nclimate and equity goals.

0 Knowledge Management
1 • The Department will be responsible for the creation of the MER Cell, which will monitor the progress of MCAP\nimplementation to meet targets, evaluate outcomes and report on KPIs.\n• The Department will have 24 climate action officers (one for each ward) and 11 climate action officers (one for\neach DMC office) to track progress and report this in the central Monitoring Information System.\n• This division will work closely with the DMC-Environment, AMC-City, and MC office to prepare a budget plan for\neach financial year, based on intended targets and progress made.\n• The MER Department will lead the biennial GHG inventory and climate risk assessment update (next scheduled\nfor 2023), and prepare an MCAP progress report every five years.
