2 Projections
3 There is high confidence that drought severity and intensity will increase in the Mediterranean. Increased evapotranspiration due
4 to growing atmospheric water demand will decrease soil moisture (high confidence). The seasonality of runoff and streamflow (the\nannual difference between the wettest and driest months of the year) is expected to increase with global warming (high confidence).\nAnnual runoff is very likely to decrease. Under middle or high-emissions scenarios, the likelihood of extreme droughts increases by\n200–300% in the Mediterranean. The paleoclimate record provides context for these future expected changes: climate change will shift\nsoil moisture outside the range of observed and reconstructed values spanning the last millennium (high confidence) (Sections 8.4.1.5\nand 8.4.1.6).
5 There is medium confidence in the increase of agricultural and ecological drought at +1.5°C, high confidence at +2°C and very likely\nat +4°C, with large decreases in soil water availability during drought events and increase in drought magnitude. There is medium\nconfidence in the increase in hydrological drought at +1.5°C, high confidence at +2°C and very likely at +4°C with very strong decrease\n(40–60%) of total runoff in the spring-summer half-year and a 50–60% increase in frequency of days under low flow (Section 11.9).
