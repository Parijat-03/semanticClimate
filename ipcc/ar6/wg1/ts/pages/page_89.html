It is evident from Table TS.5 that many heat, cold, snow and ice, coastal, and oceanic CID changes are projected with high confidence in most regions starting from a global warming level (GWL) of 2°C, indicating worldwide challenges. This section focuses on the 2°C GWL and mid-century time period because the signal emerges from natural variability for a wider range of CIDs at this higher warming level.
