3 Projections of regional monsoons during the 21st century indicate contrasting (region-dependent) and uncertain precipitation and\ncirculation changes. The annual contrast between the wettest and driest month of the year is likely to increase by 3–5% per degree\nCelsius in most monsoon regions in terms of precipitation, precipitation minus evaporation, and runoff (medium confidence). For the\nNorth American monsoon, projections indicate a decrease in precipitation, whereas increased monsoon rainfall is projected over South\nand South East Asia and over East Asia (medium confidence) (Box TS.13, Figure 1). West African monsoon precipitation is projected\nto increase over the central Sahel and decrease over the far western Sahel (medium confidence). There is low confidence in projected\nprecipitation changes in the South American and Australian-Maritime Continent regional monsoons (for both magnitude and sign)\n(Box TS.13, Figure 1). There is medium confidence that the monsoon season will be delayed in the Sahel and high confidence that it\nwill be delayed in North and South America. {8.2.2, 8.4.2.4, Box 8.2}
