TS. 5.9 Mitigation potential across sectors and systems 1
The total emission mitigation potential achievable by the year 2030, calculated based on sectoral 2 assessments, is sufficient to reduce global greenhouse gas emissions to half of the current (2019) 3 level or less (high confidence). This potential – 31 to 44 GtCO2-eq – requires the implementation of a 4 wide range of mitigation options. Options with mitigation costs lower than USD20 tCO2 -1make up 5 more than half of this potential and are available for all sectors. The market benefits of some options 6 exceed their costs. (Figure TS.23) {12.2, Table 12.3} 7
Cross-sectoral considerations in mitigation finance are critical for the effectiveness of mitigation 8 action as well as for balancing the often conflicting social, developmental, and environmental 9 policy goals at the sectoral level (medium confidence). True resource mobilisation plans that properly 10 address mitigation costs and benefits at sectoral level cannot be developed in isolation of their cross-11 sectoral implications. There is an urgent need for multilateral financing institutions to align their 12 frameworks and delivery mechanisms including the use of blended financing to facilitate cross-sectoral 13 solutions as opposed to causing competition for resources among sectors. {12.6.4} 14
