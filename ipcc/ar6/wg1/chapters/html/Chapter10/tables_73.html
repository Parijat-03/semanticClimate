5 Multiple, sometimes contrasting, lines of evidence are derived from the various data sources, methodologies and approaches\nthat can be used to construct climate information (Section 10.5 and Figure 10.1). Such data sources and methodologies include\ntheoretical understanding of relevant processes, drivers and feedbacks of climate at regional scale, observed data from multiple\ndatasets (e.g., ground station networks, satellite products, reanalysis, etc.), simulations from different model types (including general\ncirculation models (GCMs), regional climate models (RCMs), statistical downscaling methods, etc.) and experiments (e.g., Coupled\nModel Intercomparison Project Phases 5 and 6 (CMIP5 and 6), Coordinated Regional Climate Downscaling Experiment (CORDEX), and\nsingle-model initial-condition large ensembles), methodologies to attribute observed changes or events to large- and regional-scale\nanthropogenic and natural drivers and forcings as well as other relevant local knowledge (e.g., indigenous knowledge).
