Climate governance is constrained and enabled by domestic structural factors, but it is still 23 possible for actors to make substantial changes (medium evidence, high agreement). Key structural 24 factors are domestic material endowments (such as fossil fuels and land-based resources); domestic 25 political systems; and prevalent ideas, values and belief systems. Developing countries face additional 26 material constraints in climate governance due to development challenges and scarce economic or 27 natural resources. A broad group of actors influence how climate governance develop over time, 28 including a range of civic organisations, encompassing both pro- and anti-climate action groups. {13.3, 29 13.4} 30
