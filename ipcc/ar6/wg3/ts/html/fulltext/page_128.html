The relatively slow implementation of commitments by countries and stakeholders in the financial 20 sector to scale up climate finance reflects neither the urgent need for ambitious climate action, 21 nor the economic rationale for ambitious climate action (high confidence). Delayed climate 22 investments and financing – and limited alignment of investment activity with the Paris Agreement – 23 will result in significant carbon lock-ins, stranded assets, and other additional costs. This will 24 particularly impact urban infrastructure and the energy and transport sectors (high confidence). A 25 common understanding of debt sustainability and debt transparency, including negative implications of 26 deferred climate investments on future GDP, and how stranded assets and resources may be 27 compensated, has not yet been developed (medium confidence). {15.6} 28
There is a mismatch between capital availability in the developed world and the future emissions 29 expected in developing countries (high confidence). This emphasizes the need to recognize the 30 explicit and positive social value of global cross-border mitigation financing. A significant push for 31 international climate finance access for vulnerable and poor countries is particularly important given 32 these countries’ high costs of financing, debt stress and the impacts of ongoing climate change (high 33 confidence). {15.2, 15.3.2.3, 15.5.2, 15.6.1, 15.6.7} 34
Innovative financing approaches could help reduce the systemic under-pricing of climate risk in 35 markets and foster demand for investment opportunities aligned with the Paris Agreement goals. 36 Approaches include de-risking investments, robust ‘green’ labelling and disclosure schemes, in 37 addition to a regulatory focus on transparency and reforming international monetary system 38 financial sector regulations (medium confidence). Green bond markets and markets for sustainable 39 finance products have grown significantly since AR5 and the landscape continues to evolve. 40 Underpinning this evolution is investors’ preference for scalable and identifiable low-carbon investment 41 opportunities.
