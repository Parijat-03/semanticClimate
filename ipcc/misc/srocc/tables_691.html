4 There is no single approach to adaptation planning and both top-down and bottom-up approaches are widely recognised.“Institutional
5 dimensions in adaptation governance play a key role in promoting the transition from planning to implementation of adaptation”\n(Mimura et al. 2014). Adaptation is also essential at all scales, including adaptation by local governments, businesses, communities\nand individuals (Denton et al. 2014).
6 “Strengthened multi-level governance, institutional capacity, policy instruments, technological innovation and transfer and mobilisation\nof finance, and changes in human behaviour and lifestyles are enabling conditions that enhance the feasibility of mitigation and\nadaptation options for 1.5°C-consistent systems transitions” (IPCC 2018b).
7 Governance is key for vulnerability and exposure represented by institutionalised rule systems and habitualised behaviour and norms\nthat govern society and guide actors, and “it is essential to improve knowledge on how to promote adaptive governance within the\nframework of risk assessment and risk management” (Cardona 2012).
