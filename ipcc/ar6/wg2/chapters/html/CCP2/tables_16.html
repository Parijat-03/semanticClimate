0 Key governance challenges Critical enablers for C&S to address coastal hazard risk
1 Complexity: climate change compounds non-climatic hazard risks facing coastal C&S in\ninterconnected, dynamic and emergent ways for which there are no simple solutions. Draw on multiple knowledge systems to co-design and co-produce more acceptable,\neffective and enduring responses.
2 None Build governance capacity to tackle complex problems.
3 Time horizon and uncertainty: The future is uncertain, but climate change will continue\nfor generations and cannot be addressed by short-term (e.g., 1–10 years) responses alone. Adopt a long-term view but take action now. Keep options open to adjust responses as\nclimate risk escalates and circumstances change.
4 None Avoid new development commitments in exposed locations. Enable managed retreat in\nmost at-risk locations by anticipatory actions, e.g., secure funds, legal provisions for\nbuy-outs, resettlement, etc.
5 Cross-scale and cross-domain coordination: Decisions bound by jurisdictional and\nsectoral boundaries fail to address linkages within and between coastal ecosystems and C&S\nfacing interconnected climate change-compounded impacts and risk. Develop networks and linkages within and between different governance scales and levels\nand across policy domains and sectors, to improve coordination, build trust and legitimise\ndecisions.
6 None Build shared understanding and enable locally appropriate responses through\nexperimentation, innovation and social learning.
7 Equity and social vulnerability: Climate change compounds everyday inequity and\nvulnerability in coastal C&S, making it difficult to disentangle and address social drivers and\nroot causes of risk. Recognise political realities and prioritise vulnerability, justice and equity concerns to\nenable just, impactful and enduring outcomes.
8 None Strengthen community capabilities to respond to coastal hazard risk, using external\nassistance and government support if necessary.
9 Social conflict: Coastal C&S will be the focal point of contending views about appropriate\nclimate responses, and face the challenge of avoiding destructive conflict and realising its\nproductive potential. Design and facilitate tailor-made participation processes, involving stakeholders early\nand consistently from negotiating responses to implementation.
10 None Create safe arenas of engagement for inclusive, informed and meaningful deliberation\nand collaborative problem-solving.
