2 A. Croplands. Land degradation, groundwater stress and food insecurity: Soil and water\nconservation measures in the Tigray region of Ethiopia
3 In northern Ethiopia, the Tigray Region is a drought-prone area that has been subjected to severe land degradation (Frankl
4 et al. 2013) and to recurrent drought and famine during 1888–1892, 1973–1974 and 1984–1985 (Gebremeskel et al. 2018).\nThe prevalence of stunting and being underweight among children under five years is high (Busse et al. 2017) and the region\nwas again exposed to a severe drought during the strong El Niño event of 2015–2016. Croplands are the dominant land-use\ntype, with approximately 90% of the households relying on small-scale plough-based cultivation. Gullies affect nearly all slopes\nand frequently exceed 2 m in depth and 5 m in top width. Landsat imagery shows that cropland area peaked in 1984–1986, and\nincreased erosion rates in the 1980s and 1990s caused the drainage density and volume to peak in 1994 (Frankl et al. 2013).
