Sustainable development scenarios have also been developed by the Low-Carbon Society’s (LCS)
20
assessments (Kainuma et al. 2012), in which multiple sustainable development and climate change
21
mitigation goals were assessed jointly. The scenario analysis was conducted for Asian countries such
22
as South Korea, Japan, India, China and Nepal with a soft linked IAM using economy-wide and sectoral
23
models and linked to very active stakeholder engagement in order to reflect national policy perspectives
24
and priorities. Some of the models are economy-wide global IAMs, while others are national partial
25
equilibrium models.
26
27
The LCS scenarios also include a specific attempt to include ongoing dialogues with policy-makers and
28
stakeholders in order to reflect governance and enabling factors and to enable the modelling processes
29
to reflect political realism as far as possible. Diverse stakeholders who acted as validators of the
30
scientific process were included, stakeholder preferences were revealed, and recipients and users of the
31
LCS outputs were included in ongoing dialogues on outputs and in interpreting the results. The aim of
32
the stakeholder interactions was thus to fill the gap between typical laboratory-style integrated
33
modelling assessments and down-scaled but unaligned practical assessments performed at
34
disaggregated geographical and sector-specific scales.

38

39

42
