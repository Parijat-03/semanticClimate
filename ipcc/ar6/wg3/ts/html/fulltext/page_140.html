Figure TS.31 legend: chapter-level assessment for selected mitigation options. Overlaps may exist in the 5 mitigation options assessed and presented by sector and system, and feasibility might differ depending on the 6 demarcation of that option in each sector. Chapters 6, 8, 9, 10, and 12 assess mitigation response options across 7 six feasibility dimensions: geophysical, environmental-ecological, technological, economic, socio-cultural and 8 institutional. AFOLU (Ch7) and industry (Ch11) are not included because of the heterogeneity of options in 9 these sectors. For each dimension, a set of feasibility indicators was identified. Examples of indicators include 10 impacts on land use, air pollution, economic costs, technology scalability, public acceptance and political 11 acceptance (see Box TS.15, and Annex II Part IV Section 11 for a detailed explanation). An indicator could 12 refer to a barrier or an enabler to implementation, or could refer to both a barrier or an enabler, depending on the 13 context, speed, and scale of implementation. Dark blue bars indicate the extent of enablers to deployment within 14 each dimension. This is shown relative to the maximum number of possible enablers, as indicated by the light 15 blue shading. Dark orange bars indicate the extent of barriers to deployment within each dimension. This is 16 shown relative to the maximum number of possible barriers, as indicated by light orange shading. A light grey 17 dot indicates that there is limited or no evidence to assess the option. A dark grey dot indicates that one of the 18 feasibility indicators within that dimension is not relevant for the deployment of the option.
