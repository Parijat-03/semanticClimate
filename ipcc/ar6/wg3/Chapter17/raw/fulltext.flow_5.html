The landscape of transitions to sustainable development is changing rapidly, with multiple
14
transitions already underway. This creates the room to manage these transitions in ways that
15
prioritise the needs for workers in vulnerable sectors (land, energy) to secure their jobs and
16
maintain secure and healthy lifestyles, especially as the risks multiply for those exposed to heavy
17
industrial jobs and associated outcomes (medium evidence, high agreement). {17.3.2.3}. A just
18
transition incorporates key principles, such as respect and dignity for vulnerable groups, the creation of
19
decent jobs, social protection, employment rights, fairness in energy access and use, and social dialogue
20
and democratic consultation with the relevant stakeholders, while coping with the effects of asset-
21
stranding and the transition to green and clean economies (medium evidence, medium agreement). The
22
economic implications of the transition will be felt especially strongly by developing countries, with
23
high dependence on hydrocarbon products for revenue streams, as they will be exposed to reduced fiscal
24
incomes given a low demand for oil and consequent fall in oil prices (limited evidence, medium
25
agreement). {17.3.2}.

Countries with assets that are at risk becoming stranded may lack the relevant resources,
28
knowledge, autonomy or agency to reorientate, or to decide on the speed, scale and quality of the
29
transition (limited evidence, medium agreement). The urgency of mitigation might overshadow some
30
of the other priorities related to the transition, like climate change adaptation and its inherent
31
vulnerabilities. Consequently, the transition imperative could reduce the scope and autonomy for local
32
priority-setting and could ignore the additional risks in countries with a low capacity to adapt. A just
33
transition will depend on local contexts, regional priorities, the starting points of different countries in
34
the transition and the speed at which they want to travel. Both mitigation and adaptation warrant urgent
35
and prompt action given current and continuing greenhouse gas emissions and associated negative
36
impacts on humanity and ecosystems. (limited evidence, medium agreement). {17.3.2}.
