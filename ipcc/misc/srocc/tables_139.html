0 Type Knowledge gaps Understanding the uncertainties
1 Uncertainty of consequences Do the model(s) adequately represent the target system?\nWhat are the numerical values of input parameters, boundary conditions,\nor initial conditions?\nWhat are all potential events that we would take into account if we were aware\nof them? Will future events relevant for our decisions, including expected impacts\nfrom these decisions, in fact take place? Ensemble approaches; downscaling\nBenchmarking, sensitivity analyses\nScenario approaches
2 Moral uncertainty How to (ethically) evaluate the decisions?\nWhat values to base the decision on (often unreliable ranking of values not doing\njustice to the range of values at stake, see Sen 1992), including choice of discount\nrate, risk attitude (risk aversion, risk neutral, …).\nWhich ethical principles? (i.e. utilitarian, deontic, virtue, or other?). Possibly scenario analysis;\nIdentification of lock-in effects and\npath-dependency (e.g., Kinsley et al. 2016)
3 Uncertainty of demarcation What are the options that we can actually choose between? (not fully known\nbecause ‘decision costs’ may be high, or certain options are not ‘seen’ as they\nare outside current ideologies).\nHow can the mass of decisions be divided into individual decisions? e.g., how this\ninfluences international negotiations and the question who does what and when\n(cp. Hammond et al. 1999). Possibly scenario analysis
4 Uncertainty of consequences\nand uncertainty of\ndemarcation What effects does a decision have when combined with the decisions of others?\n(e.g., other countries may follow the inspiring example in climate reduction of\ncountry X, or they may use it solely in their own economic interest). Games
5 Uncertainty of demarcation\nand moral uncertainty How would we decide in the future? (Spohn 1977; Rabinowicz 2002).
