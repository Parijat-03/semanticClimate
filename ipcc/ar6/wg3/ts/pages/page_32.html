The Shared Socio-economic Pathways (SSPs)First published in 2017, the Shared Socio-economic Pathways (SSPs) are alternative projections of socio-economic developments that may influence future GHG emissions.
The initial set of SSP narratives described worlds with different challenges to mitigation and adaptation: SSP1 (sustainability), SSP2 (middle of the road), SSP3 (regional rivalry), SSP4 (inequality) and SSP5 (rapid growth). Using the SSPs permits a more systematic assessment of future GHG emissions and their uncertainties than was possible in AR5.
