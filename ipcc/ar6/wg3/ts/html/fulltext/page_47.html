Pathways accelerating action compared to current NDCs – that reduce annual GHG emissions to 14 47 (38-51) GtCO2-eq by 2030 (which is 3-9 GtCO2-eq below projected emissions from fully 15 implementing current NDCs) – make it less challenging to likely limit warming to 2°C after 2030 16 (medium confidence). The accelerated action pathways are characterized by a global, but regionally 17 differentiated, roll-out of regulatory and pricing policies. Compared to current NDCs, they describe less 18 fossil fuel use and more low-carbon fuel use until 2030; they narrow, but do not close the gap to 19 pathways that assume immediate global action using all available least-cost abatement options. All 20 delayed or accelerated action pathways likely limiting warming to below 2°C converge to a global 21 mitigation regime at some point after 2030 by putting a significant value on reducing carbon and other 22 GHG emissions in all sectors and regions. {3.5} 23
In mitigation pathways, peak warming is determined by the cumulative net CO2 emissions until 24 the time of net zero CO2 together with the warming contribution of other GHGs and climate 25 forcers at that time (high confidence). Cumulative net CO2 emissions from 2020 to the time of net 26 zero CO2 are 510 (330-710) GtCO2 in pathways that limit warming to 1.5°C with no or limited overshoot 27 and 890 (640-1160) GtCO2 in pathways likely limiting warming to 2.0°C. Rapid reductions in non-CO2 GHGs, particularly CH4, would lower the level of peak warming 31 (high confidence). Non-CO2 emissions – at the time of reaching net zero CO2 – range between 4-11 32 GtCO2-eq yr-1 in pathways likely limiting warming to 2.0°C or below. CH4 is reduced by around 20% 33 (1-46%) in 2030 and almost 50% (26-64%) in 2050, relative to 2019. CH4 emission reductions in 34 pathways limiting warming to 1.5°C with no or limited overshoot are substantially higher by 2030, 33% 35 (19-57%), but only moderately so by 2050, 50% (33-69%). CH4 emissions reductions are thus attainable 36 at comparatively low costs, but, at the same time, reductions are limited in scope in most 1.5-2°C 37 pathways. Deeper CH4 emissions reductions by 2050 could further constrain the peak warming. N2O 38 emissions are also reduced, but similar to CH4, N2O emission reductions saturate for more stringent 39 climate goals. The emissions of cooling aerosols in mitigation pathways decrease as fossil fuels use is 40 reduced. The overall impact on non-CO2-related warming combines all these factors. {3.3} 41
