The entirety of Sub-Saharan Africa has an undeveloped potential for groundwater exploitation, despite

17.3.3.3 Industry
33
Industrial transformation is a core component in achieving sustainable development. Across all
34
industrial sectors, the development and deployment of innovative technologies, business models and
35
policy approaches at scale will be essential in accelerating progress both with meeting the economic
36
and social development goals and with achieving low emissions. In this section, we assess the synergies
37
and trade-offs between mitigation options and the SDGs, with a specific focus on asking whether
38
economic growth and employment creation can work jointly with climate actions and other SDGs in
39
least developed and developing countries. Examples of synergies and trade-offs are provided based on
40
the conclusions of Chapter 9 on the building sector and Chapter 11 on industry. The potential for
41
greening industry is discussed in relation to eco-industrial parks, with examples from Ethiopia, China,
42
South Africa and Ghana.
43
44
Chapter 11 concludes that achieving net zero emissions from the industrial sector are possible. This will
45
require the provision of electricity free from greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, including from other
46
energy carriers, increased electrification, low carbon feedstocks, and a combination of energy
47
efficiency, reduced demand for materials, a more circular economy, electrification and carbon capture,
48
use and storage (CCUS).
