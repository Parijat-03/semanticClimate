Figure TS.28: Transition dynamics: levels, policies and processes. {Figure 1.7} 2
The relative importance of different ‘pillars of policy’ differs according to stage of the transition. The lower 3 panel illustrates growth of innovative technologies or practices, which if successful, emerge from niches into an 4 S-shape dynamic of exponential growth. The diffusion stage often involves new infrastructure and 5 reconfiguration of existing market and regulatory structures. During the phase of more widespread diffusion, 6 growth levels off to linear, then slows as the industry and market matures. The processes displace incumbent 7 technologies/practices which decline, initially slowly but then at an accelerating pace. Many related literatures 8 identify three main levels with different characteristics, most generally termed micro, meso and macro. 9
