The time of crossing a warming level is defined here as the midpoint of the first 20-year period during which the average\nglobal surface temperature exceeds the level. In all scenarios assessed here except SSP5-8.5, the central estimate of crossing the 1.5°C\nlevel lies in the early 2030s. This is in the early part of the likely range (2030–2052) assessed in SR1.5, which assumed continuation of\nthe then-current warming rate; this rate has been confirmed in the AR6. Roughly half of this difference arises from a larger historical\nwarming diagnosed in AR6. The other half arises because for central estimates of climate sensitivity, most scenarios show stronger\nwarming over the near term than was estimated as ‘current’ in SR1.5 (medium confidence). When considering scenarios similar to SSP1-\n1.9 instead of linear extrapolation, the SR1.5 estimate of when 1.5°C global warming is crossed is close to the central estimate reported\nhere.
