4.Observed Climate and Climate Projections
4.1Extreme Events
Extreme minimum and maximum temperature events appear to be increasing in Delhi. In December of 2006, Delhi had the lowest temperature since 1935 (0.2°C). In June 2007, Delhi had a maximum temperature of 44.9°C. While these extreme temperatures cannot be directly linked to climate change, the challenge facing Delhi is variability in weather patterns and the potential for exacerbated extreme events due to climate change. There are some months when temperature and precipitation were greater than standard deviation from the mean. The hottest summer was in 1944, the coldest winter in 1935 and the wettest monsoon in 1958, however recent years have seen similar extremes in temperature in 1978, 1988 and 1996 and precipitation in 1994, 1995, and 2003.
