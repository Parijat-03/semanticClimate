19
change mitigation. Various supply chains can be affected by climate change, energy supply and water
20

21

22
as well as by influencing outdoor environments and labour productivity (Chapter 11.1.4).

Implementing adaptation options in industry can also imply increasing the demand for packaging
24
materials such as plastics and for access to refrigeration. These are among the adaptation options that
25

An increasing number of cities are becoming involved in voluntary actions and networks aimed at
28
drawing up integrated plans for sustainable development and climate-change mitigation and adaptation,
29
including cities in both high- and low-income countries around the world. Grafakos et al. (2019);
30
Sanchez Rodriguez et al. (2018) concluded that cities are an obvious place for the development of plans
31
that can capture several synergies between sustainable development and climate-resilient pathways.
32
Kim and Grafakos (2019); and Landauer et al. (2019) similarly concluded that cities are an obvious
33
platform for the development of integrated planning efforts because of the scale of policies and actions,
34
which could potentially match the different policy domains. Kim and Grafakos (2019) assessed the level
35
of integration of mitigation and adaptation in urban climate-change plans across 44 major Latin
36
American cities, concluding that the integration of climate-change mitigation and adaption plans was
37
very weak in about half the cities and that limited donor finance was a main barrier. The authors also
38
mention barriers in relation to governance and the weakness or lack of legal frameworks. The
39
integration of SDGs with adaptation could help increase the willingness of politicians to implement
40
climate actions, as well as provide stronger arguments for investing the required resources (Sanchez
41
Rodriguez et al. 2018).

The local integration of planning and policy implementation practices was also examined by Newell et
43
al. (2018) in a study of eleven Canadian communities. It was concluded that, in order to put plans into
44
practice, a deeper understanding needs to be established of the potential synergies and trade-offs
45
between sustainable development and climate-change mitigation and adaptation. A model was applied
46
to the evaluation of key impacts, including energy innovation, transportation, the greening of cities and
47
city life. The impact assessment came to the conclusion that multiple benefits, costs and conflicting
48
areas could be involved, and that bringing a broad range of stakeholders into policy implementation
49
was therefore to be recommended.
