A growing number of countries have achieved GHG emission reductions over periods longer than 26 10 years – a few at rates that are broadly consistent with the global rates described in climate 27 change mitigation scenarios that likely to limit warming to 2°C (high confidence). At least 24 28 countries have reduced CO2 and GHG emissions for longer than 10 years. Reduction rates in a few 29 countries have reached 4% in some years, in line with global rates observed in pathways that likely limit 30 warming to 2°C.
