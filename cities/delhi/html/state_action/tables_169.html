0 As the national capital, Delhi’s urban development has been subjected to many planning
1 interventions. The Delhi Development Authority (DDA) is responsible for urban planning and
2 land management such as preparation of master plans and their implementation in coordination
3 with other organizations. One of the main aims of these plans is to deal with the rapid
4 population increase and its associated problems.
0
0 Along with planning of new urbanization, the master plan also focuses on dealing with existing
1 urban environments through various redevelopment strategies like conservation, rehabilitation
2 and slum clearance. The current master plan of Delhi, the third one, covers the period from
3 2001 to 2021 and came into effect in 2007. The current master plan focuses on issues like
4 promoting market competition in land and housing, encouraging tourism and increasing
5 revenues. Despite the existence of several master plans and the authority given to the DDA to
6 acquire the entire land within the city, a significant part of urbanization in Delhi has taken place
7 in an unplanned way. This chapter mainly deals with the Land Use, Infrastructure and Solid
8 Waste Management facilities of Delhi which constitute its Urban Planning.
