Box TS.13: Policy Attribution – Methodologies for- and estimations of- the macro-level impact 2 of mitigation policies on indices of GHG mitigation 3
Policy attribution examines the extent to which GHG emission reductions, the proximate drivers of 4 emissions, and the deployment of technologies that reduce emissions may be reasonably attributed to 5 policies implemented prior to the observed changes. Such policies include regulatory instruments such 6 as energy efficiency programmes or technical standards and codes, carbon pricing, financial support for 7 low-carbon energy technologies and efficiency, voluntary agreements, and regulation of land use 8 practices. 9
The vast majority of literature reviewed for this report examines the effect of particular instruments in 10 particular contexts {13.6, 14.3, 16.4}, and only a small number directly or plausibly infer global impacts 11 of policies. Policies also differ in design, scope, and stringency, may change over time as they require 12 amendments or new laws, and often partially overlap with other instruments. These factors complicate 13 analysis, because they give rise to the potential for double counting emissions reductions that have been 14 observed.
