0 According to the McKinsey Global Institute (2010), Africa\nhas around 60% of the global uncultivated arable land;\nthus the continent has a high potential for transformative\nchange in food production. With short and long-term climate\nchange impacts combined with local poverty conditions,\nland degradation and poor farming practices, Africa cannot\ngrow enough food to feed its rapidly growing population.\nSustainable improvement of productivity is essential, even as\nthe impacts of climate change on food security in Africa are\nprojected to be multiple and severe.
1 Sustainable Land Management (SLM) of farming systems\nis important to address climate change while dealing with\nthese daunting food security needs and the necessity to\nimprove access to nutritious food to maintain healthy and\nactive lives in Africa (AGRA 2017). SLM has functions beyond\nthe production of food, such as delivery of water, protection\nagainst disease (especially zoonotic diseases), the delivery of\nenergy, fibre and building materials.
2 Commodity-based systems – driven by external markets –\nare increasing in Africa (cotton, cocoa, coffee, palm oil,\ngroundnuts) with important impacts on the use of land\nand climate. Land degradation, decreasing water resources,\nloss of biodiversity, excessive use of synthetic fertilisers\nand pesticides are some of the environmental challenges\nthat influence preparedness to adapt to climate change\n(Pretty and Bharucha 2015). A balanced strategy on African\nagriculture can be based on SLM and multifunctional\nland-use approaches combining food production, cash crops,\necosystem services, biodiversity conservation, ecosystem
