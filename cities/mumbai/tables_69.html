1 Population having no access to\nmass transit within 1km\nGreater Mumbai: 39.5%\nLow rank ward: M/E 74.6%\nTop rank ward: B 0.8% Access to mass transit\nenables access to jobs and\nresources, which can help\nimprove household incomes.\nWomen and children have\naccess to safe and affordable\nnetworks for better access to\njobs and education, thereby\nincreasing their adaptive\ncapacity and reducing\nvulnerability. More than 60% of Mumbai’s population lives\nwithin walking distance of 1km from mass\ntransit stations and has good access to\nsustainable mobility options. (This analysis takes\ninto consideration the completion of the Metro\nnetwork, planned for the next decade).\nDuring flood events, waterlogged streets hinder\nphysical access to mass transit stations,\ndisrupting regular access to mobility networks.\nDuring a flooding event, the population\nhaving access to a mass transit station within\n1km potentially declines to 36.6%, as compared\nwith 60.5% on normal days.\nThe populations that are dependent on mass\ntransit to access jobs and for personal needs are\ndeeply impacted due to loss of working hours,\nreduced productivity, loss of income and the\ninability to access basic needs or access health\nand emergency services.\nThe vulnerability of poor households is\nexacerbated during such events.
