Box TS.1 | The COVID-19 Pandemic: Impact on Emissions and Opportunities for Mitigation
The COVID-19 pandemic triggered the deepest global economic contraction as well as CO2emission reductions since the Second World War {2.2.2}. While emissions and most economies rebounded in 2020, some impacts of the pandemic could last well beyond this.
Starting in the spring of 2020 a major break in global emissions trends was observed due to lockdown policies implemented in response to the pandemic. Overall, global CO2-FFI emissions are estimated to have declined by 5.8% (5.1–6.3%) in 2020, or about 2.2 (1.9–2.4%) GtCO2in total. This exceeds any previous global emissions decline since 1970 both in relative and absolute terms (Box TS.1, Figure 1).
