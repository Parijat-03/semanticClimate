Box TS.2: GHG emission metrics provide simplified information about the effects of different 15 greenhouse gases 16
Comprehensive mitigation policy relies on consideration of all anthropogenic forcing agents, which 17 differ widely in their atmospheric lifetimes and impacts on the climate system. GHG emission metrics 18 provide simplified information about the effect that emissions of different gases have on global 19 temperature or other aspects of climate, usually expressed relative to the effect of emitting CO2ǂ. This 20 information can support choices about priorities, trade-offs and synergies in mitigation policies and 21 emission targets for non-CO2 gases relative to CO2 as well as baskets of gases expressed in CO2-eq. 22
The choice of metric can affect the timing and emphasis placed on reducing emissions of Short-Lived 23 Climate Forcers (SLCFs) relative to CO2 within multi-gas abatement strategies as well as the costs of 24 such strategies. Different metric choices can also alter the time at which net zero GHG emissions are 25 calculated to be reached for any given emissions scenario. A wide range of GHG emission metrics has 26 been published in the scientific literature, which differ in terms of: (i) the key measure of climate change 27 they consider, (ii) whether they consider climate outcomes for a specified point in time or integrated 28 over a specified time horizon, (iii) the time horizon over which the metric is applied, (iv) whether they 29 apply to a single emission pulse, to emissions sustained over a period of time, or to a combination of 30 both, and (v) whether they consider the climate effect from an emission compared to the absence of that 31 emission, or compared to a reference emissions level or climate state {Annex I}. 32
Parties to the Paris Agreement decided to report aggregated emissions and removals (expressed as CO2-33 eq) based on the Global Warming Potential with a time horizon of 100 years (GWP100) using values 34 from IPCC AR5 or from a subsequent IPCC report as agreed upon by the CMA†, and to account for 35 future nationally determined contributions (NDCs) in accordance with this approach. Parties may also 36 report supplemental information on aggregate emissions and removals, expressed as CO2-eq, using 37 other GHG emission metrics assessed by the IPCC. 38
The WG III contribution to AR6 uses updated GWP100 values from AR6 WG I to report aggregate 39 emissions and removals unless stated otherwise. These reflect updated scientific understanding of the 40 response of the climate system to emissions of different gases and include a methodological update to 41 incorporate climate-carbon cycle feedbacks associated with the emission of non-CO2 gases (see Annex 42 II Part II Section 8 for a list of GWP100 metric values). The choice of GWP100 was made inter alia for 43 consistency with decisions under the Rulebook for the Paris Agreement and because it is the dominant 44 metric used in the literature assessed by WG III.
