The global-scale projections (Figure CB6.1) are consistent with results from regional-scale studies using more sophisticated models. Kraaijenbrink et al. (2017) projected mass losses for all glaciers in High Mountain Asia of 64 ± 5% (RCP8.5) by the end of the century (2071–2100) compared to 1996–2015. A high-resolution regional glaciation model including ice dynamics indicated that by 2100 glacier volume in western Canada will shrink by ~70% (RCP2.6) to ~90% (RCP8.5) relative to 2005 (Clarke et al., 2015). Zekollari et al. (2019) projected that the glaciers in the European Alps will largely disappear by 2100 (94 ± 4% mass loss relative to 2017) for RCP8.5, while projected mass losses are 63 ± 11% for RCP2.6.
AR5 concluded with high conﬁdence that due to a pronounced imbalance between current glacier mass and climate, glaciers are expected to further recede even in the absence of further climate change. Studies since AR5 agree and provide further evidence (Mernild et al., 2013; Marzeion et al., 2018).
