Most countries were forced to undertake unprecedented levels of short-term public expenditures in 13 2021. This is expected to slow economic growth and may squeeze financial resources for mitigation 14 and relevant investments in the near future. Pandemic responses have increased sovereign debt across 15 countries in all income bands and the sharp increase in most developing economies and regions has 16 caused debt distress, widening the gap in developing countries’ access to capital. {15.6.3} 17
The wider overall reduction in energy investment has prompted a relative shift towards low carbon 18 investment particularly for major future investment decisions by the private sector {15.2.1, 15.3.1, 19 15.6.1}. Some countries and regions have prioritised green stimulus expenditures for example as part 20 of a ‘Green New Deal’ {Box 13.1}. This is motivated by assessments that investing in new growth 21 industries can boost the macroeconomic effectiveness (‘multipliers’) of public spending, crowd-in and 22 revive private investment, whilst also delivering on mitigation commitments. {15.2.3} 23
