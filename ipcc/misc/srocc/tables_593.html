1 Biomass in 2013 contributed about 60 EJ (10%) to global primary energy4 (WBA 2016). In 2011, the IPCC Special Report on Renewable\nEnergy Sources concluded that biomass supply for energy could reach 100–300 EJ yr–1 by 2050 with the caveat that the technical\npotential5 cannot be determined precisely while societal preferences are unclear; that deployment depends on ‘factors that are\ninherently uncertain’; and that biomass use could evolve in a ‘sustainable’ or ‘unsustainable’ way, depending on the governance\ncontext (IPCC 2012). The IPCC WGIII AR5 report noted, in addition, that high deployment levels would require extensive use of\ntechnologies able to convert lignocellulosic biomass such as forest wood, agricultural residues, and lignocellulosic crops. 7 Different Integrated Assessment Models (IAMs) use alternative approaches to land allocation when determining where and how\nmuch biomass is used, with some relying on economic approaches and some relying on rule-based approaches (Popp et al. 2014).\nDespite these differences, a consistent finding across models is that increasing biomass supply to the extent necessary to support\ndeep decarbonisation is likely to involve substantial land-use change (Popp et al. 2017) (Cross-Chapter Box 9 in this chapter). In\nmodel runs, bioenergy deployment and the consequent demand for biomass and land, is influenced by assumptions around the price\nof bioenergy, the yield of bioenergy crops, the cost of production (including the costs of fertiliser and irrigation if used), the demand\nfor land for other uses, and the inclusion of policies (e.g., subsidies, taxes, constraints) that may alter land-use or bioenergy demand.\nIn general, higher carbon prices result in greater bioenergy deployment (Cross-Chapter Box 7, Figure 1, right panel) and a larger\npercentage of BECCS. Other factors can also strongly influence bioenergy use, including the cost and availability of fossil fuels (Calvin\net al. 2016a), socio-economics (Popp et al. 2017), and policy (Calvin et al. 2014; Reilly et al. 2012).
