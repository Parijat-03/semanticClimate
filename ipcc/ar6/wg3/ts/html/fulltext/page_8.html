TS. 2 The changed global context, signs of progress and continuing 1 challenges 2
Since the IPCC’s Fifth Assessment Report (AR5), important changes that have emerged include 3 the specific objectives established in the Paris Agreement of 2015 (for temperature, adaptation 4 and finance), rising climate impacts, and higher levels of societal awareness and support for 5 climate action (high confidence). Meeting the long-term temperature goal in the Paris Agreement, 6 however, implies a rapid inflection in GHG emission trends and accelerating decline towards ‘net zero’. 7 This is implausible without urgent and ambitious action at all scales. {1.2, 1.3, 1.5, 1.6, Chapters 3 and 8 4} 9
Effective and equitable climate policies are largely compatible with the broader goal of 10 sustainable development and efforts to eradicate poverty as enshrined in the UN 2030 Agenda for 11 Sustainable Development and its 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), notwithstanding 12 trade-offs in some cases (high confidence). Taking urgent action to combat climate change and its 13 impacts is one of the 17 SDGs (SDG13). However, climate change mitigation also has synergies and/or 14 trade-offs with many other SDGs. There has been a strong relationship between development and GHG 15 emissions, as historically both per capita and absolute emissions have risen with industrialisation. 16 However, recent evidence shows countries can grow their economies while reducing emissions. 17 Countries have different priorities in achieving the SDGs and reducing emissions as informed by their 18 respective national conditions and capabilities. Given the differences in GHG emissions contributions, 19 degree of vulnerability and impacts, as well as capacities within and between nations, equity and justice 20 are important considerations for effective climate policy and for securing national and international 21 support for deep decarbonisation. Achieving sustainable development and eradicating poverty would 22 involve effective and equitable climate policies at all levels from local to global scale. Failure to address 23 questions of equity and justice over time can undermine social cohesion and stability.
