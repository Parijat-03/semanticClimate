The World in 2050 Initiative (TWI) includes a comprehensive assessment of technologies, economies
33
and societies embodied in the SDGs (IIASA 2018). The assessment addresses social dynamics,
34
governance and sustainable development pathways within the areas of human capacity and
35
demography, consumption and production, decarbonization and energy, food, the biosphere and water,
36
smart cities and digitalization. The report concludes that the 17 SDGs are integrated and complementary
37
and need to be addressed in unison. Studies using global IAMs that were presented in the GEO6 report
38
(United Nations Environment Programme 2019, Chapter 22) concluded that transitions to low-carbon
39
pathways will require a broad portfolio of measures, including a mixture of technological
40
improvements, lifestyle changes and localized solutions. The many different challenges require
41
dedicated measures to improve access to, for example, food, water and energy, while at the same time
42
reducing the pressure on environmental resources and ecosystems. A key contribution may be a
43
redistribution of access to resources, where both physical access and affordability play a role. The IAMs
44
cover large countries and regions, and localized solutions are not properly addressed in the modelling
45
results. This implies that, for example, trade-offs between energy access and affordability are not fully
46
represented in aggregate modelling results.
