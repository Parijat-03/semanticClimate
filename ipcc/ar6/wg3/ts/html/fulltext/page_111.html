18 Carbon leakage is a cross-sectoral and cross-country consequence of differentiated climate policy 19 (robust evidence, medium agreement). Carbon leakage occurs when mitigation measures implemented 20 in one country/sector leads to increased emissions in other countries/sectors. Global commodity value 21 chains and associated international transport are important mechanisms through which carbon leakage 22 occurs. Reducing emissions from the value chain and transportation can offer opportunities to mitigate 23 three elements of cross-sectoral spill-overs and related leakage: 1) domestic cross-sectoral spill-overs 24 within the same country; 2) international spill-overs within a single sector resulting from substitution 25 of domestic production of carbon-intensive goods with their imports from abroad; and 3) international 26 cross-sectoral spill-overs among sectors in different countries. {12.6.3} 27
