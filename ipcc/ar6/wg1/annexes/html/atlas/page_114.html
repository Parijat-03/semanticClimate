With the increased use of satellite and 44 newly available remote sensing data, the observational dataset for glaciers is becoming more detailed and 45 new inventories that gather the monitoring efforts are enabling leaps in the state of knowledge of glaciers. 46 The coming few years will see huge improvements in monitoring of glaciers in the Arctic and in the world. 47 The data presented in AR6 are made available in open access from Global Terrestrial Network for Glaciers 48 (GTN-G), which provides an umbrella for existing and operational monitoring services and is jointly run by 49 three operational bodies – the World Glacier Monitoring Service (WGMS), the US National Snow and Ice 50 Data Center (NSIDC), and the Global Land Ice Measurements from Space (GLIMS) initiative. 51 52 The WGMS and its predecessor organizations have internationally coordinated glacier monitoring since 53 1894, resulting in an unprecedented database on glacier changes in length, area, volume and mass with the 54 longest time series reaching back to the 16th century (Zemp et al., 2015; WGMS, 2017).
