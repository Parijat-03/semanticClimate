Figure TS.21 legend: Mitigation response options related to demand for services have been categorised into 4 three domains: ‘socio-cultural factors’, related to social norms, culture, and individual choices and behaviour; 5 ‘infrastructure use’, related to the provision and use of supporting infrastructure that enables individual choices 6 and behaviour; and ‘technology adoption’, which refers to the uptake of technologies by end users. 7 Potentials in 2050 are estimated using the International Energy Agency’s 2020 World Energy Outlook STEPS 8 (Stated Policy Scenarios) as a baseline. This scenario is based on a sector-by-sector assessment of specific 9 policies in place, as well as those that have been announced by countries by mid-2020. This scenario was 10 selected due to the detailed representation of options across sectors and sub-sectors. 11 The heights of the coloured columns represent the potentials on which there is a high level of agreement in the 12 literature, based on a range of case studies. The range shown by the dots connected by dotted lines represents the 13 highest and lowest potentials reported in the literature which have low to medium levels of agreement. 14 The demand side potential of socio-cultural factors in the food system has two parts. The economic potential of 15 direct emissions (mostly non-CO2) demand reduction through socio-cultural factors alone is 1.9 GtCO2eq 16 without considering land use change by diversion of agricultural land from food production to carbon 17 sequestration. If further changes in land use enabled by this change in demand are considered, the indicative 18 potential could reach 7 GtCO2eq. 19 The electricity panel presents separately the mitigation potential from changes in electricity demand and 20 changes associated with enhanced electrification in end use sectors. Electrification increases electricity demand, 21 while it is avoided though demand-side mitigation strategies. Load management refers to demand side flexibility 22 that can be achieved through incentive design like time of use pricing/monitoring by artificial intelligence, 23 diversification of storage facilities etc. NZE (IEA Net Zero Emissions by 2050 Scenario) is used to compute the 24 impact of end use sector electrification, while the impact of demand side response options is based on bottom-up 25 assessments. 26 Dark grey columns show the emissions that cannot be avoided through demand-side mitigation options. 27 The table indicates which demand-side mitigation options are included.
