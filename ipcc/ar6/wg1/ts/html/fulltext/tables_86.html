7 With continued global warming, it is likely that global land monsoon precipitation will increase during this century (Box TS.13, Figure 1),\nparticularly in the Northern Hemisphere, although the monsoon circulation is projected to weaken. A slowdown of the tropical circulation\nwith global warming can partly offset the warming-induced strengthening of precipitation in monsoon regions (high confidence). In the\nnear term, global monsoon changes are likely to be dominated by the effects of internal variability and model uncertainties (medium\nconfidence). In the long term, global monsoon rainfall change will feature a robust north–south asymmetry characterized by a greater\nincrease in the Northern Hemisphere than in the Southern Hemisphere and an east–west asymmetry characterized by enhanced Asian–\nAfrican monsoons and a weakened North American monsoon (medium confidence). {4.4.1, 4.5.1, 8.4.1}
