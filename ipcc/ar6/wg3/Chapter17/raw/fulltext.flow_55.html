17.4.1
Behavioural and lifestyle changes
39
Transitions toward more sustainable development pathways are both an individual and a collective
40
challenge, requiring an examination of the role of values, attitudes, beliefs and structures that shape
41
behaviour, and of the dynamics of social movements and education at the local community, regional
42
and global levels. Labelling the carbon included in products, for example, could help the decision-
43
making process and increase awareness and knowledge. Individual action suggests aggregated but
44
uncoordinated actions taken by individuals, whereas collective sustainability actions involve
45
coordination, a process of participation and governance that may ensure more efficient, equitable and
46
effective outcomes. There is evidence that the behaviour of individuals and households are part of a
47
more encompassing collective action (see also Chapter 5.4.1).
