17.3.3.6 Cross-sectoral digitalization
13
In this section, the potential role of digitalization as a facilitator of a fast transition to sustainable
14
development and low emission pathways is assessed based on sectoral examples. The contributions of
15
digital technology could contribute to efficiency improvements, cross-sectoral coordination, including
16
new IT services, and decreasing resource use, implying several synergies with the SDGs, as well as
17
trade-offs, for example, in relation to reduced employment, increasing energy demand and the
18
increasing demand for services, possibly increasing GHG emissions.
19
20
The COVID-19 pandemic caused radical temporary breaks with past energy use trends. How post-
21
pandemic recovery will impact on the longer-term energy transition is unclear. Recovering from the
22
pandemic with energy-efficient practices embedded in new patterns of travel, work, consumption and
23
production reduces climate mitigation challenges (Kikstra et al. 2021). The potential of digital contact-
24
tracing to slow the spread of a virus had been quietly explored for over a decade before the COVID-19
25
pandemic thrust the technology into the spotlight (Cebrian 2021). The COVID-19 crisis is among the
26
most disruptive events in recent decades and has had consequences for consumer behaviour. During the
27
lockdowns in most countries, consumers have turned to online shopping for food products, personal
28
hygiene and disinfection (Cruz-Cárdenas et al. 2021), making society more digitally literate.
29
30
The cost of new services provided by digitalization can be high, and this could imply barriers for low-
31
income countries in joining new global information sharing systems and markets. Altogether this
32
implies that any assessment of the contribution of digitalization to support the SDGs and low-carbon
33
pathways will only be able to provide very context-specific results. Digital technologies could
34
potentially disrupt production processes in nearly every sector of the economy. However, as an
35
emerging area experiencing the rapid penetration of many sectors, there could be a window of
36
opportunity for integrating sustainable development and low emission pathways. IIASA (2020)
37
concludes that the digital revolution is characterised by many innovative technologies, which can create
38
both synergies and trade-offs with the SDGs (IIASA 2020).
