0 Reduce Restore
1 • The BMC is currently developing a city-level SWM\nplan and has launched an online citizen survey under\nits Vision 2030 initiative.\n• Property tax relief of 5-10% is provided to residential\nsocieties that segregate and compost waste.\n• A total of 19 organic waste convertors has been\nproposed for vegetable and fruit markets.\n• In 2018, BMC issued an order, mandating bulk\nwaste generators to set up biodegradable waste\ncomposting units. A total of 3,367 additional bulk\nwaste generators have been notified, and 50% of\nthe bulk generators identified by BMC are currently\ncomposting waste at source. • Dumpsite reclamation is taking place at Mulund, with\n3,35,150 MT of legacy waste having been disposed\ntill 2021.\n• Waste-to-energy projects having a total capacity of\n600 TPD and 4MW energy generation have been\nproposed for Deonar. A total of 1,200 TPD of W2E\nprojects are in the pipeline.\n• A total of INR 5,600 per unit per month has been\nallocated for waste management in slums under\nSMPA. An eligible organization is awarded an area\nbetween 5 and 18 units. The contract for this work is\nrenewed annually.
2 Recover Recycle
3 • A total of 85 hectares of land has been allotted for\nscientific waste.\n• The BMC has tied up with recyclers, and a rebate of\nup to 10% on property tax is being offered to housing\nsocieties that segregate waste.\n• Decentralized community-level composting and\nbiomethanation is being promoted.\n• M/s. Godrej Industries has begun 10 TPD green\nwaste processing pelletisation in Ghatkopar (N ward)\nthrough its CSR division and processes 3-4 MT of\ngreen waste per day.\n• A total of 32 parks in the city have compost pits\nacross all wards; In the last quarter of 2017, 3,285 kg\nof compost was generated here. • Currently, the city has 46 dry waste segregation\ncenters handled by waste and ragpickers\nassociations. In 2019, 188.5 TPD of dry waste was\nmanaged. Plastic shredding machines are installed at\na few of these centers.\n• BMC is planning to undertake collection,\ntransportation, processing and disposal of 1,200 TPD\nof C&D waste (BMC, 2021).\n• Under Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR),\ncompanies such as Bisleri and Coca Cola are setting\nup plastic processing units in the city.\n• BMC has proposed that MPCB be appointed the\nauthorized e-waste recycling agency to set up\ne-waste collection centers in wards.
